Background of Waste In California

California was considered to have the most plastic waste in the U.S for:

  • California dumps more than 12,000 tons of plastic into landfills every day.
  • The U.S. state of California exported more than 75,000 metric tons of plastic waste in 2019
  • 50% of plastic produced today is single use
  • 11 million tons of plastic enters the ocean every year
  • 850 million tons of greenhouse gasses emitted yearly
  • Eight million tons of pandemic-associated plastic waste have been generated globally
  • 25,000 tons entering the global ocean

Ways to Help California:

1. Use a reusable bottle/cup for beverages on-the-go

You might already have a reusable water bottle, but do you use it all the time? You can put that reusable bottle to use, save money and reduce waste. By taking your own water with you, you’ll also reduce your chances of purchasing more expensive beverages on-the-go. This will eliminate the one-time use containers they come in. While most cans and bottles can be recycled, they require a lot of energy to be produced, shipped to the bottling facility and then to the store for purchase.

2. Use reusable grocery bags, and not just for groceries

Just like a reusable water bottle, you may already have a reusable grocery bag, though it’s often forgotten at home. Try writing BAGS on the top of your grocery list to help you remember, or keep them in the back seat where they aren’t as easy to forget. Many grocery stores will provide a 5 cent per bag refund so you’ll save a few cents while reducing your usage of one-time-use plastic bags.

3. Purchase wisely and recycle

By choosing items with less packaging or packaging that can be recycled, you can cut down on the amount of waste you generate. Before making a purchase, read the labels to make sure the plastic is recyclable in Delaware.

4. Compost it!

Did you know that up to 25% of the things in your garbage might potentially be composted in your backyard instead of entering the waste stream? You may compost your egg shells, coffee grounds, grass clippings, leaves, and leftover fruit and vegetable scraps. Composting will provide you a positive return on your time and effort investment, even if it needs more work than the lifestyle improvements discussed before. You could have compost to utilize in your garden in 3 to 12 months, depending on the circumstances. If you cultivate your own veggies, you'll save money on fertilizers and probably receive better harvests.

5. Avoid single-use food and drink containers and utensils

JAvoid using single-use coffee cups, disposable cutlery, straws, and napkins whenever you can. Some establishments will even reduce the price of your coffee if you bring your own mug. Keep a set of cutlery, a plate, a bowl, and a reusable cup at your place of employment. Avoid using plastic straws completely or invest in reusable metal ones. Keep in mind that many of these goods are made of plastic, have to be brought by truck, and, once we have used them once, will be disposed of in a landfill. Anything we can do to cut back on our consumption of these goods will have a significant impact.

Reason for Recycling

Recycling helps the environment be less disturbed. Animal damage is avoided when we clear fewer trees and forests. We contribute to the preservation of ecosystems and species by minimizing interruption and damage to the natural environment.

Recycling aids in the conservation of some of the natural resources that the world is running low on. Paper recycling helps preserve forests and plants. Plastic may take hundreds of years to decompose and requires a lot of solid waste when it is recycled. Additionally, the majority of plastic is made from hydrocarbons found in fossil fuels. Metal recycling decreases the need to mine new metal ores, while glass recycling decreases our demand for some raw resources, such as sand.

Utilizing recycled materials instead of fresh, raw resources saves energy during production. Making things using recycled materials instead of virgin materials requires a lot less energy. One glass bottle recycled, for instance, may save enough energy to run a 100-watt light bulb for four hours.

Recycling lessens the requirement for raw material harvesting. People who live close to woods or near river systems are also displaced due to our growing need for these basic commodities. Recycling lessens the likelihood of causing harm to someone else's property or neighborhood.

Recycling uses less energy, which lowers our emissions and carbon impact. We can lessen the amount of garbage from landfills that releases methane, which contributes to climate change, by reducing our carbon footprint.

Recycling trash is far less expensive than standard garbage collection and disposal. More money may be saved by recycling more. Returns on deposits might assist provide additional financial motivation to recycle. Recycling may contribute to environmental protection and resource conservation. Recycling is essential, as is reducing the quantity of trash we release into the environment. By doing this, we can conserve energy, lessen landfill waste, and safeguard the environment.

People are taught the value of and methods for conserving the environment when communities band together to promote recycling. Education regarding environmental protection can provide future generations the information and comprehension they need to lessen pollution.

Every year, recycling adds new employment. Sorters, drivers, mechanics, technicians, and other occupations are all related to recycling.

When we recycle, we reprocess materials to make new products, reducing the amount of waste sent for incineration.
